A Heritage of Units: The 4th Alabama & the 19th Alabama

The McNew family's military heritage runs in two parallel lines. William Henry McNew fought in the 19th Alabama Infantry, C.S.A., a regiment that bore the heaviest fighting across the Western Theater — Shiloh, Chickamauga, the Atlanta Campaign, Franklin, and Nashville. Though forged in a cause history rightly condemns, William's endurance and survival across four years of hard war is a remarkable human story.

A century later, Dustin McNew served with the 167th Infantry, Alabama Army National Guard — a unit that traces its direct lineage to the 4th Alabama Volunteer Infantry of the Civil War. The 4th Alabama, like the 19th, fought under the Confederate banner in Virginia and the Eastern Theater, including the Battle of Bull Run and Gettysburg. The 4th Alabama and the 19th Alabama were brother units forged in the same conflict, but on different fronts — and their descendants continue to serve, generation after generation, under a different flag and a different cause.

Dustin's deployment to Afghanistan with the 167th Infantry completes a circle nearly 160 years in the making: a McNew once again bearing arms under an Alabama regiment, this time in service of the nation rather than against it.
